Mid Bio (150+-)

Félix Albertos Marco works at the University of Castilla-La Mancha as an assistant professor (accredited as associate professor). He teaches subjects related with Human-Computer Interaction and Software Design within the Degree in Computer Science. His research is focused on understand how users interact with computers, mainly through the use of Web technologies. He has coordinated the Degree on Digital Interaction Techniques and Computation in the University of Lleida, where he worked in a number of other degrees and masters. He received his PhD with his thesis entitled Responsive Web Interaction from the University of Castilla-La Mancha in 2017. There, he showed how Internet has made interaction with Web applications possible from almost anywhere and using any device, addressing the definition and management of interactive events, considering connectivity to the system and multi-device configuration. He has published his work in many international conferences, book chapters, as well as in 6 JCR journals. Many of then are related to the area of human computer interaction, education, health and software engineering. He has performed research stays at the University Paul Sabatier (Toulouse, France) and the Université Côte d’Azur (Sophia Antipolis, France). He has participated in 7 research projects (2 ongoing).
